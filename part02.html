FLAKES FILES - PART 02

The October Comeback

After months of disappearing, Flakes, also known as James, suddenly came crawling back into the SJC community in October. Everyone was shocked, because the last time he left, he burned half the server on his way out. But there he was again, in his same pastel skirts, thigh highs, cat ears, and fake uwu personality, acting like nothing ever happened.

James kept reminding people he was 22 and turning 23 soon, like that somehow made him wiser or more responsible. But honestly he acted nothing like an adult. People like Noom, Staroflight, Itz_dev, Malb, and a bunch of others instantly noticed the same strange energy around him. He acted sweet, but in the same fake, over-the-top way he always had.

He came back claiming he wanted to “help the community,” but everyone could tell something was off. He didn’t return for friends. He returned for clout.

The Fake Giveaways for Popularity

When James showed up again, the first thing he did was host giveaways. But not because he wanted to be generous. He wanted to regain popularity, rebuild his reputation, and climb back up the SJC ranks.

He advertised everything as “big events hosted by Flakes,” like he was some celebrity returning from a world tour. Every giveaway announcement felt like a promo for himself, not the actual prize.

People like ZylozVA, tripl3tsgamer, Lexie, Zonez, Val, KindlyVernon, Seppgen, CXI, Jens, noah, chloe, Morse, ronaldo, leanne, Bugz, and Malb all noticed the same thing. He only hosted events when it benefited him. If it didn’t boost his popularity or make people praise him, he didn’t bother.

It was obvious he wasn’t helping the community. He was trying to make himself look like a hero.

The Source of His Robux

James made himself look rich, but everyone eventually found out that none of the Robux he gave away was his own.
Almost all of it came from offshore 15-year-old farmers who grinded PLS DONATE on Roblox all day. They funneled their earnings through alts and sold it under the table to people like James.

On top of that, James was constantly begging rich players for money. The list was long too.
He begged Haz3mn, Zenuux, Vrevix, Sri, Quinn, Rox, rrwxva, PrinseElias, Inviz, Vexezifps, and Shlopiezz nonstop.

He didn’t befriend any of them for actual friendship. He used them for their wallets.

Even Malb said that whenever James messaged him, it was always about needing “a little boost” or “help reaching a goal,” never an actual conversation.

James acted like the Robux was his, but everyone knew it wasn’t. It was farmed, begged for, or manipulated out of wealthier players.

Manipulating His Way Back Up

As October went on, James got more aggressive with trying to rebuild his reputation. He showed up to every SJC event, acted overly friendly, and pretended he cared about the community. But people who knew him before saw through it instantly.

Noom kept warning people that James was doing it all for attention.
Staroflight said she recognized the same patterns he had before he left.
Itz_dev straight up said he wasn’t fooled for a second.
Even Malb, who usually stays neutral, said that James felt “different but not in a good way.”

James tried to convince newer members that he was some big positive influence in SJC, while older members remembered the scams, lies, and drama he caused.

He didn’t want forgiveness.
He wanted power.
He wanted status.
He wanted everyone to say “Flakes is back and better than ever.”

Even though everyone knew he was still the same manipulative dude under the surface.

The Truth Behind Flakes’ “Return”

By the end of the month, the truth was obvious. James didn’t return because he missed the community. He didn’t return to fix things or apologize.

He came back to rebuild his image and get back to the top.
Every event he hosted was for attention.
Every giveaway was for popularity.
Every conversation he had was calculated.
Every rich player he messaged was another wallet he wanted access to.

People like Lexie, Zonez, Val, Malb, and Bugz all confirmed the same thing. Nothing about his return was genuine.

James might have rebranded himself as Flakes again, but he was still the same person who used everyone, begged for Robux, cut off friends, and tried to climb ranks through manipulation instead of honesty.

And the worst part was that he genuinely believed nobody remembered what he did.



But the SJC community remembered everything.
